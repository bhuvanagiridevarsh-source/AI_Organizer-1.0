What the chart actually tells you

✅ Your real moat: nobody in this list touches your files

Read the top two rows again. Zero of the ten funded "memory/context layer" companies actually organize, move, or rename files on your disk. They ingest data to feed other AI tools. You're the only one whose core action is "drag a messy folder in, get it sorted." That is a different job-to-be-done, and it's one normal (non-developer) people viscerally understand and want.

Translation: the scary funded competitors you were worried about mostly aren't competing for the same user. They sell plumbing to developers; you sell a clean desktop to humans.

⚠️ But you have a different competitor set you didn't list

Your true head-to-head rivals are local AI file organizers: AI File Sorter (open-source, Win/Mac/Linux, local GGUF), Tiny Sort (Mac App Store, on-device), Sortio (offline mode), and The Drive AI (cloud, multi-drive). These do the same core job you do.

Here's the good news: most of them only sort files. They don't have your profile-building, chat-with-files, learning-from-corrections, or Work Mode compliance layer. So your edge isn't "AI organizes files" (taken) — it's "local file organizer that also becomes a private brain about your work." That combination is genuinely rare.

🔧 Where you're behind — and it's fixable

The whole right side of the matrix (browser extension, MCP server, public API) is where the funded crowd is strong and you're not yet. That's fine — those are distribution features, not your core value. But the single highest-leverage gap is the "injects context into other AI tools" row: you have an in-app prompt enhancer, but no live extension that feeds your profile into ChatGPT/Claude the way Supermemory and MemoryPlugin do. If you ever want to claim the "context layer" story too, that's the one to build. Until then, lead with the file-organizer story — it's stronger and less crowded.

🎯 The one-sentence positioning this chart suggests

"The private, on-device AI that cleans up your files and quietly learns what you work on — so your computer and your AI tools both finally understand you."

Lead with privacy + local (your strongest, most defensible claim vs. the cloud crowd), wedge in through file organization (a pain people already feel), expand into the context layer later.
